English Listening Practice Test - B1 Level

Instructions: This test has three parts. For each part, listen to the audio carefully. You can play each audio up to 4 times only. Then choose the best answer for each question.

Part 1 - Tips for Managing Money

Audio 1: How to manage your money

Remaining plays: 4

1. What is the main purpose of the talk?

2. How many tips does the speaker plan to share?

3. According to the speaker, what is the most important tip?

4. What example does the speaker give to show that "small changes" can make a difference?

5. What does the speaker say about your salary?

6. How many other companies does the speaker suggest checking to compare your salary?

7. According to the speaker, who needs a budget?

8. What does the speaker mean by "don't just write it and forget about it"?

Part 2 - Movie Preferences

Audio 2: People talking about movies

Remaining plays: 4

9. What kind of movies does Sue like?

10. What kind of movies does Sue dislike?

11. What kind of movies does Bob like best?

12. Does Bob like westerns?

13. What is Andrew's favorite type of movie?

14. Does Andrew like comedies?

15. What kind of movies does Tina love?

16. Does Tina like horror movies?

Part 3 - Kloze-Zone Store Feedback (Matching)

Audio 3: Six people talking about Kloze-Zone store

Remaining plays: 4

17 - 22. Match each speaker with the correct topic.

Listen to the six speakers in Column A. Read the six topics in Column B. For each speaker, choose the number of the topic they are talking about.

Column A — Speakers

Column B — Topics

  1. The mobile app is excellent, but more staff are needed to help customers in the fitting rooms.
  2. Working hours are too long; some colleagues already left because of this.
  3. Window displays should be more creative and innovative to attract people walking by.
  4. Marketing campaigns should also target male customers, not only women.
  5. Staff turnover is very high; the company should improve its brand awareness.
  6. The store should organize more special events and promotions, especially in quiet months.
